My Top Country Artists

Morgan Wallen, country music singer and songwriter, was born on May 13th, 1993, in Sneedsville, Tennessee. While growing up, Morgan aspired to play baseball in college but after an elbow injury, he chose to pursue music. In 2014, Morgan Wallen competed in Season 6 of The Voice, but was eliminated during playoffs. He then went on to release his first EP in 2015. While still co-creating music with other country artists, Morgan was working on albums of his own, realeasing his debut album If I Know Me, in 2018. It was in August of 2020 that If I Know Me reached #1 on the Top Country Albums chart. After that, Morgan released Dangerous: The Double Album which also remained atop the Billboard 200 chart. This album became the only country album in the 64-year history of the Billboard 200 to spend its first 7 weeks in the number 1 spot. My top 3 favorite Morgan Wallen songs include:

  1. Whiskey Glasses
  2. More Suprised Than Me
  3. You Proof

Kameron Marlowe, country singer and songwriter, was born on June 20th, 1997, in Kannapolis, North Carolina. Kameron first became interested in country music after listening to the radio with his grandfather. He sang in church growing up, and then formed a band in high school. While attending college, Kameron's mother fell ill so he quit school to help support and care for her. He continued to puruse music, catching the attention of a talent who recuited him to be on Season 15 of The Voice. After his time on the voice he moved to Nashville where he released his first deput song, Giving You Up, in 2019. He kept producing music, releasing a handful of singles, including Girl On Fire, all of which appear on his debut album We Were Cowboys in 2022. My top 3 favorite Kameron Marlowe songs include:

  1. Giving You Up
  2. Steady Heart
  3. Girl On Fire

Luke Combs, country singer and songwriter, was born on March 2nd, 1990, in Charlotte, North Carolina. Growing up, Luke enjoyed playing football and singing in vocal groups. Luke attended Appalachian State college for a period of time but decided to but his studies to the side and focus more on his music career. He later went on to drop out of college, just before earning his degree. After playing many gigs with a lot of the songs he wrote, he decided to head to Nashville in 2014. In February of 2014, Combs self- released his first EP. It was after his release of his song Hurricane in 2015, that secured him a contract. Combs went on to release more EPs that then helped set the stage for the release of his full debut album, This One's For You, which debuted at numnber one on Billboard's Country Albums. It was Lukes release of his song Beautiful Crazy, that helped him earn distinction of being the first artist to simultaneously hold the top position on all five of Billboard's main country charts: Top Country Albums, Hot Country Songs, Country Airplay, Country Streaming Songs, and Country Digital Song Sales. Since, Luke has continued to release singles and albums that continue to take the charts.

  1. Beautiful Crazy
  2. Forever After All
  3. One Number Away
